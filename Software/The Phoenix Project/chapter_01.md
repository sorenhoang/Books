**Chapter 1 Summary**

- **Date:** Tuesday, September 2.
- **Protagonist:** Bill Palmer, the Director of Midrange Technology Operations at Parts Unlimited.
- **The Shake-up:** Bill is summoned by Laura Beck (VP of HR) and learns that both the CIO (Luke) and the VP of IT Operations (Damon) have been fired effective that morning,.
- **The Promotion:** CEO Steve Masters offers Bill the position of VP of IT Operations. Bill initially tries to refuse the job, viewing it as a career-ender due to the high turnover of IT executives and the company's instability,,.
- **Company Context:** Parts Unlimited is in severe financial trouble; stock prices have plummeted, and competitors are outmaneuvering them. The board has given Steve six months to make dramatic improvements or face removal,.
- **Project Phoenix:** The company's survival hinges on "Project Phoenix," a delayed initiative intended to close the gap with competitors by integrating retail and e-commerce channels.
- **The Mandate:** Steve manipulates Bill into accepting the role by appealing to his sense of duty and the company's survival. Steve's directive for IT Operations is reliability ("keep the lights on") so the business can focus on shipping Phoenix,.
- **Immediate Crisis:** The chapter ends with Bill facing his first major incident: a payroll system failure that threatens to leave thousands of factory workers unpaid by the 5 p.m. deadline,.
